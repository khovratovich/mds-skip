\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

Zero-knowledge proof systems---SNARKs, STARKs, and Bulletproofs---have become core infrastructure for privacy-preserving computation. Their inner loops are dominated by hash computations that must be expressed natively over prime fields with as few multiplications as possible. The Poseidon family of hash functions~\cite{poseidon,poseidon2}, built on the Substitution-Permutation Network (SPN) design, is the dominant answer: deployed in Filecoin, Mina, Plonky3, and many other production systems, it achieves the best known trade-off between algebraic complexity and security.

An SPN permutation operates on a state $W \in \FFF_p^t$ by alternating a non-linear S-box layer $\SSS$ (applying the power map $x \mapsto x^d$ to each element) with a linear diffusion layer $\LLL$ (multiplication by a $t \times t$ matrix $M$). Security is measured primarily through the \emph{Constrained-Input Constrained-Output} ($\CICO$) problem: given partial constraints on the input and output of the permutation, find a satisfying assignment. $\CICO$ hardness underlies collision resistance and preimage resistance, and concretizes the security of the Poseidon Initiative bounty challenges~\cite{poseidoninitiative}.

\emph{Round-skipping attacks} reduce the $\CICO$ problem by algebraically bypassing a prefix of rounds. Merz and Rodr{\'{i}}guez Garc{\'{i}}a~\cite{skip2026} recently showed that the structured linear layers of Poseidon2(b)---optimized for hardware efficiency but not MDS---admit skipping attacks that bypass up to $\approx t/4$ full rounds by spending roughly four degrees of freedom per skipped round. This raises a fundamental open question: \emph{how many rounds can be skipped when the linear layer is an MDS matrix?} Poseidon1 uses MDS matrices (via the Cauchy or circulant construction, achieving branch number $b = t+1$), so this question directly bounds the attack surface of the Poseidon Initiative bounty instances.

\subsection*{Our Contributions}

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Skipping trail framework (Section~\ref{sec:trails}).}
    We introduce \emph{skipping trails}: truncated differential trails satisfying an algebraic compatibility condition that forces the ratio of active state element pairs to propagate linearly through the S-box layer. A valid $m$-round skipping trail certifies that a structured family of inputs maps through $m$ rounds to a predictable output set, enabling the adversary to reduce a $\CICO$ instance on the full permutation to one on the remaining $R_F - m$ full rounds and $R_P$ partial rounds.

    \item \textbf{Tight bounds on round skipping (Section~\ref{sec:bounds}).}
    We prove that an $m$-round skipping trail can exist only if the branch number $b$ of $\LLL$ satisfies
    \[
        b \;\leq\; \frac{4t}{m} \quad (m \text{ even}), \qquad b \;\leq\; \frac{4t - 2}{m - 1} \quad (m \text{ odd}).
    \]
    For MDS matrices ($b = t+1$), this rules out skipping $m \geq 4$ rounds for all $t \geq 1$. For $m = 3$, the bound gives $b \leq 2t-1$, which MDS satisfies, so three-round skipping is not excluded.

    \item \textbf{Concrete trails for Poseidon1-KoalaBear (Section~\ref{sec:concrete}).}
    For the canonical bounty instance ($p = 2^{31} - 2^{24} + 1$, $d = 3$, $t = 16$, $R_F = 8$, $R_P = 20$), we exhibit explicit 2-round skipping trails for both the circulant and Cauchy MDS constructions. We further search for 3-round skipping trails; a positive result would constitute the first example of three-round MDS round skipping.

    \item \textbf{Complexity analysis and implementation (Section~\ref{sec:impl}).}
    We quantify the $\CICO$ cost reduction as a function of the trail width and $R_P$. We provide an implementation that finds skipping trails for concrete Poseidon1-KoalaBear matrices and demonstrates the round-skipping attack on the bounty instances.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Scope.}
We analyze skipping within the full-round layer only ($m \leq R_F$). Extending skipping into the partial-round layer is qualitatively different and is left for future work.

\subsection{Related Work}
\label{sec:related}

\paragraph{Poseidon hash functions.}
Poseidon1~\cite{poseidon} (Grassi, Khovratovich, Rechberger, Roy, Schofnegger, 2021) is an SPN hash function over $\FFF_p$ with $R_F$ \emph{full rounds} (all $t$ S-boxes active) flanking $R_P$ \emph{partial rounds} (one S-box active), and an MDS linear layer with branch number $b = t+1$. Poseidon2~\cite{poseidon2} replaces the MDS matrix with a structured matrix achieving better circuit efficiency; this matrix is not MDS in general, and its reduced diffusion is the structural weakness exploited in~\cite{skip2026}.

\paragraph{Round-skipping attacks.}
The idea of bypassing SPN rounds algebraically has roots in the \emph{rebound attack} of Mendel, Rechberger, Schl{\"a}ffer, and Thomsen~\cite{rebound} on AES-based hash functions. In the rebound attack, the adversary satisfies differential constraints through the middle rounds by working inward from both sides simultaneously; the adversary controls the degrees of freedom through the linear (MDS) layer and the non-linear S-box.
Merz and Rodr{\'{i}}guez Garc{\'{i}}a~\cite{skip2026} adapted this idea to Poseidon2 by identifying truncated differentials with a ratio-preserving property through the power-map S-box. Their attack skips up to $\approx t/4$ rounds and yields the first algebraic collision attack that outperforms the corresponding preimage attack on Poseidon2. They explicitly leave open the question of how far round skipping can go for MDS-based SPNs; our paper answers this question.

\paragraph{Algebraic attacks.}
Algebraic attacks on Poseidon-type primitives include Gr{\"o}bner-basis methods, linearization, and interpolation. These complement round-skipping attacks: our results show that, for MDS matrices, at most 3 full rounds can be skipped (with $m \geq 4$ being provably impossible), bounding the scope of the round-reduction phase in any hybrid attack.

\paragraph{MDS matrices and branch numbers.}
The branch number $b = \min_{x \neq 0}(\mathrm{wt}(x) + \mathrm{wt}(Mx))$ quantifies the diffusion of a linear layer, where $\mathrm{wt}$ denotes Hamming weight. MDS matrices achieve $b = t+1$, the maximum by the Singleton bound. The Cauchy construction $M[i][j] = (x_i - y_j)^{-1}$ yields an MDS matrix over any prime field with $p > 2t$; circulant MDS matrices, used in Plonky3 and the Poseidon Initiative bounty instances, achieve the same branch number with a more regular structure. The distinction between the two constructions becomes relevant in Section~\ref{sec:concrete}.

\section{Preliminaries and Notation}
\label{sec:prelim}

\subsection{SPN Permutations}

Let $\FFF = \FFF_p$ be a prime field and $t \geq 2$ an integer. An \emph{SPN permutation} $\PPP$ operates on the state $W \in \FFF^t$ as a composition of $R$ rounds. Each round $i$ applies three operations in sequence:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item $\LLL$ — a linear transformation that multiplies the state by a fixed matrix $M \in \FFF^{t \times t}$;
    \item $\SSS$ — a non-linear substitution layer that applies the power map $x \mapsto x^d$ coordinate-wise;
    \item $\CCC_i$ — a constant-addition layer that adds a round-specific vector of constants to the state.
\end{enumerate}
We write the $i$-th round as
\[
    W_i^{(A)}\xrightarrow{\LLL}W_i^{(B)}\xrightarrow{\SSS}W_i^{(C)}\xrightarrow{\CCC_i}W_{i+1}^{(A)}.
\]
Here $W_i^{(A)}$ is the state at the beginning of round $i$ (after constants of round $i-1$), $W_i^{(B)}$ is the state after the linear layer, and $W_i^{(C)}$ is the state after the S-box layer.

\subsection{The CICO Problem}

The primary security notion for SPN-based hash functions is the \emph{Constrained-Input Constrained-Output} ($\CICO$) problem.

\begin{definition}[$\CICO$]
    Given an SPN permutation $\PPP : \FFF^t \to \FFF^t$ and affine constraints
    \[
        \mathcal{C}_{\mathrm{in}} \subseteq \FFF^t, \quad \mathcal{C}_{\mathrm{out}} \subseteq \FFF^t,
    \]
    find $W \in \mathcal{C}_{\mathrm{in}}$ such that $\PPP(W) \in \mathcal{C}_{\mathrm{out}}$, or determine that no such $W$ exists.
\end{definition}

The $\CICO$ problem underlies collision and preimage resistance of Poseidon-based hash functions. Concretely, fixing the capacity elements of the input and output yields a collision; fixing the output fully yields a preimage. The Poseidon Initiative bounty challenges~\cite{poseidoninitiative} are cast as $\CICO$ instances.

\subsection{Poseidon1}
\label{sec:poseidon1}

Poseidon1~\cite{poseidon} is an SPN permutation over $\FFF_p^t$ with a \emph{mixed-round} structure: it consists of $R_F$ \emph{full rounds}, in which the S-box layer is applied to all $t$ state elements, flanking $R_P$ \emph{partial rounds}, in which only one designated element is cubed. Concretely, the round schedule is $R_F/2$ full rounds, followed by $R_P$ partial rounds, followed by $R_F/2$ full rounds; the total number of rounds is $R = R_F + R_P$.

The canonical bounty instance over the KoalaBear field uses the parameters
\[
    p = 2^{31} - 2^{24} + 1, \quad d = 3, \quad t = 16, \quad R_F = 8, \quad R_P = 20.
\]

\paragraph{Scope.}
This paper analyzes round skipping within the full-round layers only ($m \leq R_F$). Extending skipping into the partial-round layer is qualitatively different and is left for future work.

\subsection{MDS Matrices and Branch Number}
\label{sec:mds}

\begin{definition}[Branch number]
    The \emph{branch number} of a linear map $\LLL$ with matrix $M$ is
    \[
        b(M) = \min_{x \neq 0}\bigl(\mathrm{wt}(x) + \mathrm{wt}(Mx)\bigr),
    \]
    where $\mathrm{wt}(v)$ denotes the Hamming weight (number of non-zero coordinates) of $v \in \FFF^t$.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[MDS matrix]
    A matrix $M \in \FFF^{t \times t}$ is \emph{MDS} (Maximum Distance Separable) if $b(M) = t + 1$, the maximum allowed by the Singleton bound.
\end{definition}

An equivalent characterization: $M$ is MDS if and only if every square submatrix of $M$ is non-singular.

Two MDS constructions appear in deployed instances of Poseidon1.
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Cauchy MDS.} For distinct elements $x_1,\ldots,x_t,y_1,\ldots,y_t \in \FFF_p$ with $x_i \neq y_j$ for all $i,j$, the matrix $M[i][j] = (x_i - y_j)^{-1}$ is MDS over any prime field $p > 2t$.
    \item \textbf{Circulant MDS.} A circulant matrix $M$ defined by its first row $(c_0, c_1, \ldots, c_{t-1})$ with $M[i][j] = c_{(j-i) \bmod t}$ is MDS for specific choices of $(c_0,\ldots,c_{t-1})$ depending on $p$ and $t$. The Poseidon Initiative bounty instances and Plonky3 use circulant MDS matrices.
\end{itemize}
Both constructions achieve $b = t + 1$; the distinction becomes relevant in Section~\ref{sec:concrete}.


\section{Truncated Differentials and Skipping Trails}
\label{sec:trails}

A round-skipping attack exploits the observation that a structured family of inputs
---an affine coset---can be propagated through a sequence of SPN rounds while
remaining an affine coset at the output.  The S-box layer is normally the obstacle:
it maps an affine family to a non-affine image.  Our skipping trail framework
identifies the precise algebraic condition on the differential matrices and the
center of the coset that forces the S-box to collapse the coset back to a new affine
family.  We build up the formalism in three layers: propagation through the linear
layer (truncated differential), propagation through the S-box (S-compatibility),
and the full multi-round structure (skipping trail).

\begin{definition}
    Let $1\leq k\leq t$.
    A set $\mathcal{W}\subseteq\FFF^t$ \emph{satisfies the matrix differential~$\mathbf{A}$},
    centered at $W_0\in\FFF^t$, if $\mathbf{A}\in\FFF^{t\times k}$ and
    \[
        \mathcal{W} = \bigl\{\,W_0 + \mathbf{A}\cdot\mathbf{X} : \mathbf{X}\in\FFF^k\,\bigr\}.
    \]
    We call $\mathcal{W}$ a \emph{differential coset} of~$\mathbf{A}$; the integer
    $k$ is the \emph{width} of the differential and $\dim(\mathcal{W}) = \mathrm{rank}(\mathbf{A})$.
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}
    The matrix $\mathbf{A}\in\FFF^{t\times k}$ plays two roles: its \emph{row support}
    (the set of indices $j$ with a non-zero $j$-th row) determines which state coordinates
    are free, while the rank of $\mathbf{A}$ determines the dimension of the affine subspace.
    Taking $k=t$ and $\mathbf{A}$ full-rank recovers the whole state space; taking $k=1$
    gives an affine line.
\end{remark}

\begin{definition}
    A \emph{truncated differential} of width $k$ is a pair $(\mathbf{A},\mathbf{B})$
    with $\mathbf{A},\mathbf{B}\in\FFF^{t\times k}$ for some $1\leq k\leq t$ such that
    \[
        \mathbf{A} \xrightarrow{\LLL}\mathbf{B},
        \quad\text{i.e.\ }\mathrm{Im}(M\mathbf{A}) = \mathrm{Im}(\mathbf{B}),
    \]
    where $M$ is the matrix of $\LLL$.
    The indices $j$ with a non-zero $j$-th row of $\mathbf{B}$ are called the \emph{active positions}.
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}
    The condition $\mathrm{Im}(M\mathbf{A}) = \mathrm{Im}(\mathbf{B})$ means that both
    $M\mathbf{A}$ and $\mathbf{B}$ span the same $\mathrm{rank}(\mathbf{A})$-dimensional
    coordinate subspace of $\FFF^t$.  For an MDS matrix $M$, the active positions of
    $\mathbf{A}$ (non-zero rows) and of $\mathbf{B}$ must have the same count, both
    equal to $\mathrm{rank}(\mathbf{A})$.
    When $\mathbf{B}$ is chosen to be diagonal this recovers the classical formulation;
    the general $\FFF^{t\times k}$ form allows a basis for the output subspace that need
    not be aligned with coordinate axes.
\end{remark}

\begin{definition}
    A matrix~$\mathbf{B}\in\FFF^{t\times k}$ is \emph{$\SSS$-compatible} with a matrix~$\mathbf{A}'\in\FFF^{t\times k'}$
    if there exist differential cosets $\mathcal{W}^{(B)}$ of~$\mathbf{B}$ and
    $\mathcal{W}^{(A)}$ of~$\mathbf{A}'$ such that
    \[
        \SSS\!\left(\mathcal{W}^{(B)}\right) = \mathcal{W}^{(A)},
    \]
    where $\SSS$ applies the map $x\mapsto x^d$ coordinatewise.
    (Round constants shift the center of $\mathcal{W}^{(A)}$ and do not affect $\SSS$-compatibility.)
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}
    A $m$-round \emph{truncated differential trail} is a sequence of truncated differentials
    $\{(\mathbf{A}_i,\mathbf{B}_i)\}_{i=1}^{m}$ such that for every $i=1,\ldots,m-1$,
    $\mathbf{B}_i$ is $\SSS$-compatible with~$\mathbf{A}_{i+1}$.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}\label{def:arity2}
    A matrix $\mathbf{B}\in\FFF^{t\times k}$ is \emph{arity-2} if
    \begin{itemize}
        \item every column of $\mathbf{B}$ has exactly $2$ nonzero entries, and
        \item every row of $\mathbf{B}$ has at most $1$ nonzero entry.
    \end{itemize}
    The two rows $\ell_j < r_j$ carrying the nonzero entries of column $j$ form a \emph{pair}; we write $L = \{\ell_1,\ldots,\ell_k\}$ and $R = \{r_1,\ldots,r_k\}$ for the resulting sets of left and right indices (disjoint by the row condition).
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}\label{def:skipping-trail}
    An $m$-round \emph{skipping trail} is an $(m+1)$-round truncated differential
    trail $\{(\mathbf{A}_i,\mathbf{B}_i)\}_{i=1}^{m+1}$ such that
    \begin{enumerate}
        \item for every $i$ from $1$ to $m$, $\mathbf{B}_i$ is arity-2, and
        \item there exists a sequence of centers
        $\{W_i^{(A)}, W_i^{(B)}, W_i^{(C)}\}_{i=1}^{m+1}$ propagating through the trail,
        i.e.\ $W_i^{(B)} = M\,W_i^{(A)}$, $W_i^{(C)} = \SSS(W_i^{(B)})$, and
        $W_{i+1}^{(A)} = W_i^{(C)} + c_i$ for round constants $c_i$.
    \end{enumerate}
\end{definition}
\subsection{A Worked Example: $t=4$, $M=\mathrm{circ}(2,1,1,1)$}
\label{sec:example}

We exhibit a concrete 1-round skipping trail ($m=1$) for the circulant matrix
\[
    M = \mathrm{circ}(2,1,1,1) =
    \begin{pmatrix}2&1&1&1\\1&2&1&1\\1&1&2&1\\1&1&1&2\end{pmatrix}, \quad t=4,\;k=1,\;d=3.
\]

\paragraph{Differential matrices.}
Set $\mathbf{A}_1 = \mathbf{B}_1 = \mathbf{A}_2 = \mathbf{B}_2 = (0,1,-1,0)^T \in \FFF^{4\times 1}$.
One checks directly that
\[
    M\,(0,1,-1,0)^T = (0\cdot 2+1\cdot 1+(-1)\cdot 1, \; 1\cdot 2+(-1)\cdot 1, \;
                       1\cdot 1+(-1)\cdot 2, \; 1\cdot 1+(-1)\cdot 1)^T
    = (0,1,-1,0)^T,
\]
so $\mathbf{A}$ is an eigenvector of $M$ with eigenvalue $1$.  Hence
$\mathrm{Im}(M\mathbf{A}_1) = \mathrm{Im}(\mathbf{A}_1) = \mathrm{Im}(\mathbf{B}_1)$,
confirming that $(\mathbf{A}_1, \mathbf{B}_1)$ is a valid truncated differential.

\paragraph{Arity-2 check.}
The single column of $\mathbf{B}_1=(0,1,-1,0)^T$ has exactly two nonzero entries (at positions
$\ell=1$ and $r=2$ using 0-based indexing), and each row is nonzero in at most one column.
Thus $\mathbf{B}_1$ is arity-2 with pair $(\ell,r)=(1,2)$.

\paragraph{Ratio condition.}
For $d=3$ and the pair $(\ell,r)=(1,2)$:
\[
    \left(\frac{\mathbf{B}_1[2,1]}{\mathbf{B}_1[1,1]}\right)^{\!3}\mathbf{A}_2[1,:]
    = (-1)^3 \cdot 1 = -1 = \mathbf{A}_2[2,:]. \quad\checkmark
\]

\paragraph{Center constraint for $\SSS$-compatibility.}
The input coset $\mathcal{W}^{(B)}_1 = \{W_0 + X(0,1,-1,0)^T : X\in\FFF\}$ has
\[
    (W_0[0],\; W_0[1]+X,\; W_0[2]-X,\; W_0[3]).
\]
After cubing coordinate-wise (with $u = W_0[1]+X$):
\[
    \bigl(W_0[0]^3,\;u^3,\;(W_0[2]-X)^3,\;W_0[3]^3\bigr).
\]
For the image to be a coset of $\mathbf{A}_2=(0,1,-1,0)^T$ we need $(W_0[2]-X)^3 = -u^3$ for all $X$.
Setting $c = W_0[1]+W_0[2]$ gives $W_0[2]-X = c-u$, so $(c-u)^3=-u^3$ for all $u$ requires $c=0$, i.e.
\[
    W_0[1] + W_0[2] = 0.
\]
Under this constraint $(W_0[2]-X)^3 = (-u)^3 = -u^3$, and the output coset is
\[
    \bigl\{\,(W_0[0]^3,\;W_0[1]^3+Z,\;-W_0[1]^3-Z,\;W_0[3]^3)\,:\,Z\in\FFF\,\bigr\},
\]
which is a coset of $(0,1,-1,0)^T = \mathbf{A}_2$.  $\SSS$-compatibility is confirmed.

\paragraph{Summary.}
The sequence $(\mathbf{A}_1,\mathbf{B}_1)=(\mathbf{A}_2,\mathbf{B}_2)=(0,1,-1,0)^T$ forms
a valid 1-round skipping trail.  Any center $W_0$ with $W_0[1]+W_0[2]=0$ satisfies
the compatibility condition, leaving the values $W_0[0], W_0[1], W_0[3]$ free.
By Theorem~\ref{thm:skipping}, the affine line
$\{W_0 + X(0,1,-1,0)^T\}$ maps through one full round to another affine line,
reducing the $\CICO$ problem to a univariate system over the remaining rounds
(Corollary~\ref{cor:cico}).

\medskip

With an $m$-round skipping trail we can algebraically bypass the first $m$ full rounds
of the SPN, reducing the effective $\CICO$ instance. The following theorem formalizes
this and Section~\ref{sec:bounds} derives tight bounds on how large $m$ can be.
\section{Theoretical Bounds on Round Skipping}
\label{sec:bounds}

\begin{theorem}\label{thm:skipping}
    Let $\{(\mathbf{A}_i,\mathbf{B}_i)\}_{i=1}^{m+1}$ be an $m$-round skipping trail
    with propagating centers $\{W_i^{(A)},W_i^{(B)},W_i^{(C)}\}$.
    For every $i\in\{1,\ldots,m\}$ and every column $j$ of $\mathbf{B}_i$
    with arity-2 pair $(\ell_j,r_j)$, denote $\rho_{i,j}=\mathbf{B}_i[r_j,j]/\mathbf{B}_i[\ell_j,j]$
    and suppose:
    \begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*)]
        \item \emph{Matrix ratio condition:}
        \begin{equation}\label{eq:ratio}
            \rho_{i,j}^d\,\mathbf{A}_{i+1}[\ell_j,\,:]\;=\;\mathbf{A}_{i+1}[r_j,\,:];
        \end{equation}
        \item \emph{State ratio condition:}
        \begin{equation}\label{eq:state-ratio}
            W_i^{(B)}[r_j]\;=\;\rho_{i,j}\,W_i^{(B)}[\ell_j].
        \end{equation}
    \end{enumerate}
    Then
    \begin{equation}\label{eq:skipping2}
        \bigl\{\,\PPP^m\bigl(W_1^{(A)}+\mathbf{A}_1\cdot\mathbf{X}\bigr)\,\bigr\}_{\mathbf{X}\in\FFF^k}
        \;=\;
        \bigl\{\,W_{m+1}^{(A)}+\mathbf{A}_{m+1}\cdot\mathbf{X}'\,\bigr\}_{\mathbf{X}'\in\FFF^k}.
    \end{equation}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
    We prove by induction on $i\in\{1,\ldots,m\}$ that after $i$ full rounds the image of
    $\{W_1^{(A)}+\mathbf{A}_1\mathbf{X}\}_{\mathbf{X}\in\FFF^k}$ equals
    $\{W_{i+1}^{(A)}+\mathbf{A}_{i+1}\mathbf{X}'\}_{\mathbf{X}'\in\FFF^k}$
    for a bijective reparameterization on $\FFF^k$.

    \textit{Inductive step.}
    Assume the image after $i-1$ rounds is $\{W_i^{(A)}+\mathbf{A}_i\mathbf{X}\}$
    (the base case $i=1$ is the hypothesis).

    \textit{Linear layer.}
    Since $(\mathbf{A}_i,\mathbf{B}_i)$ is a truncated differential,
    $\mathrm{Im}(M\mathbf{A}_i)=\mathrm{Im}(\mathbf{B}_i)$, so
    $M\mathbf{A}_i=\mathbf{B}_i\mathbf{T}_i$ for an invertible $\mathbf{T}_i\in\FFF^{k\times k}$.
    With $\mathbf{Y}=\mathbf{T}_i\mathbf{X}$:
    \[
        \LLL\bigl(\{W_i^{(A)}+\mathbf{A}_i\mathbf{X}\}\bigr)
        = \{W_i^{(B)}+\mathbf{B}_i\mathbf{Y}\}.
    \]

    \textit{S-box layer.}
    Let $v=W_i^{(B)}+\mathbf{B}_i\mathbf{Y}$.
    Since $\mathbf{B}_i$ is arity-2, for column $j$ with pair $(\ell_j,r_j)$
    and $b_j=\mathbf{B}_i[\ell_j,j]$:
    \[
        v[\ell_j]=W_i^{(B)}[\ell_j]+b_j Y_j,\qquad
        v[r_j]  =W_i^{(B)}[r_j]+\rho_{i,j}\,b_j Y_j.
    \]
    By the state ratio condition~\eqref{eq:state-ratio},
    $W_i^{(B)}[r_j]=\rho_{i,j}W_i^{(B)}[\ell_j]$, hence
    $v[r_j]=\rho_{i,j}\,v[\ell_j]$.
    Setting $Z_j=v[\ell_j]^d-W_i^{(C)}[\ell_j]$ and using
    $W_i^{(C)}[r_j]=(\rho_{i,j}W_i^{(B)}[\ell_j])^d=\rho_{i,j}^d W_i^{(C)}[\ell_j]$:
    \[
        \SSS(v)[\ell_j]=W_i^{(C)}[\ell_j]+Z_j,\qquad
        \SSS(v)[r_j]  =W_i^{(C)}[r_j]+\rho_{i,j}^d Z_j.
    \]
    For any inactive row $s$ (zero row in $\mathbf{B}_i$),
    $v[s]=W_i^{(B)}[s]$ is constant, so $\SSS(v)[s]=W_i^{(C)}[s]$.
    Defining $\mathbf{C}_i\in\FFF^{t\times k}$ by $\mathbf{C}_i[\ell_j,j]=1$,
    $\mathbf{C}_i[r_j,j]=\rho_{i,j}^d$, and zero elsewhere:
    \[
        \SSS\bigl(\{W_i^{(B)}+\mathbf{B}_i\mathbf{Y}\}\bigr)
        =\{W_i^{(C)}+\mathbf{C}_i\mathbf{Z}\}.
    \]

    \textit{Identification with $\mathbf{A}_{i+1}$.}
    By $\SSS$-compatibility (Definition~3), any inactive row $s$ of $\mathbf{B}_i$
    fixes coordinate $s$ under $\SSS$, so $\mathbf{A}_{i+1}[s,:]=\mathbf{0}$ for
    all inactive~$s$.  Hence $\mathbf{A}_{i+1}$ has support only on $L_i\cup R_i$.
    The matrix ratio condition~\eqref{eq:ratio} then gives
    $\mathbf{A}_{i+1}[r_j,:]=\rho_{i,j}^d\mathbf{A}_{i+1}[\ell_j,:]$ for each~$j$,
    which means $\mathrm{Im}(\mathbf{C}_i)=\mathrm{Im}(\mathbf{A}_{i+1})$.
    Hence $\mathbf{C}_i=\mathbf{A}_{i+1}\mathbf{S}_i$ for an invertible
    $\mathbf{S}_i\in\FFF^{k\times k}$; with $\mathbf{X}'=\mathbf{S}_i\mathbf{Z}$:
    \[
        \SSS\bigl(\{W_i^{(B)}+\mathbf{B}_i\mathbf{Y}\}\bigr)
        =\{W_i^{(C)}+\mathbf{A}_{i+1}\mathbf{X}'\}.
    \]

    \textit{Round constant.}
    Since $W_{i+1}^{(A)}=W_i^{(C)}+c_i$, the image after round~$i$ is
    $\{W_{i+1}^{(A)}+\mathbf{A}_{i+1}\mathbf{X}'\}$, completing the induction.
    Equation~\eqref{eq:skipping2} follows by taking $i=m$.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[$\CICO$ degree reduction]\label{cor:cico}
    Let $m\leq R_F$ and suppose there exists an $m$-round skipping trail
    $\{(\mathbf{A}_i,\mathbf{B}_i)\}_{i=1}^{m+1}$.
    Suppose the $\CICO$ input constraint defines a differential coset of $\mathbf{A}_1$,
    and let the composition of the remaining $s = R - m$ rounds of $\PPP$,
    viewed as a function of the state at the beginning of round $m+1$,
    be expressed by polynomials of degree at most $D$ over $\FFF$.
    Then the $\CICO$ problem reduces to a system of polynomial equations of
    degree at most $D$.
\end{corollary}
\begin{proof}
    By Theorem~\ref{thm:skipping}, the image of the input differential coset
    under the first $m$ rounds is the differential coset
    $\{W_{m+1}^{(A)} + \mathbf{A}_{m+1}\cdot\mathbf{X}'\}_{\mathbf{X}'\in\FFF^k}$.
    The remaining $s$ rounds are a polynomial map of degree $D$ applied to this
    affine family; substituting the parameterization by $\mathbf{X}'$ turns the
    $\CICO$ output constraints into polynomial equations in $\mathbf{X}'$ of
    degree at most $D$.
    (The number of free variables equals the rank of $\mathbf{A}_{m+1}$, i.e.
    the number of active positions of $\mathbf{B}_{m+1}$.)
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Zero rows and support of $M^{-1}$]\label{lem:branch}
    Let $(\mathbf{A},\mathbf{B})$ be a truncated differential for a linear layer $\LLL$ with matrix $M$.
    Suppose $\mathbf{B}$ is diagonal with $k$ zeros. Then any zero row of $\mathbf{A}$ corresponds to a row of $M^{-1}$ with at least $t-k$ zeros in the positions where $\mathbf{B}$ is nonzero.
    In particular, the number of zero rows in $\mathbf{A}$ is at most the number of rows of $M^{-1}$ with support contained in the zero pattern of $\mathbf{B}$.
    For an MDS matrix, all rows of $M^{-1}$ are fully dense, so $\mathbf{A}$ can have at most $k$ zero rows.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
    Since $\mathbf{A} = M^{-1}\mathbf{B}$, the $i$-th row of $\mathbf{A}$ is $e_i^T M^{-1} \mathbf{B}$, where $e_i$ is the $i$-th standard basis vector.
    If $\mathbf{A}_{i,*} = 0$, then $e_i^T M^{-1} \mathbf{B} = 0$, i.e., the $i$-th row of $M^{-1}$ is supported only on the zero coordinates of $\mathbf{B}$ (since multiplying by a nonzero diagonal entry would yield a nonzero entry).
    Thus, for each zero row in $\mathbf{A}$, the corresponding row of $M^{-1}$ has support contained in the zero pattern of $\mathbf{B}$, i.e., at least $t-k$ zeros in the nonzero positions of $\mathbf{B}$.
    For MDS, all rows of $M^{-1}$ are fully dense, so this is only possible if the row is multiplied by all zeros in $\mathbf{B}$, i.e., at most $k$ zero rows in $\mathbf{A}$.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}
    This lemma refines the branch-number argument: for MDS matrices, the only way for $\mathbf{A}$ to have a zero row is for $\mathbf{B}$ to have a zero in the same position. For non-MDS matrices, the structure of $M^{-1}$ can allow more zero rows in $\mathbf{A}$ if $M^{-1}$ has sparse rows aligned with the zero pattern of $\mathbf{B}$.
\end{remark}

\begin{corollary}[Consecutive active S-boxes]\label{cor:consecutive}
    In an $m$-round skipping trail with linear layer of branch number $b$, for all
    $i\in\{1,\ldots,m\}$,
    \begin{equation}\label{eq:consecutive}
        n_{B_i} + n_{B_{i+1}} \geq b,
    \end{equation}
    where $n_{B_j}$ denotes the number of active positions of $\mathbf{B}_j$.
    In particular, the total number of active S-box applications satisfies
    \[
        \sum_{i=1}^{m} n_{B_i} \;\geq\; \left\lceil \frac{mb}{2} \right\rceil.
    \]
\end{corollary}
\begin{proof}
    S-compatibility (Definition~3) forces the active positions of $\mathbf{A}_{i+1}$
    to be a subset of those of $\mathbf{B}_i$, giving $n_{A_{i+1}}\leq n_{B_i}$.
    Lemma~\ref{lem:branch} applied to $(\mathbf{A}_{i+1},\mathbf{B}_{i+1})$ gives
    $n_{A_{i+1}}+n_{B_{i+1}}\geq b$, hence $n_{B_i}+n_{B_{i+1}}\geq b$.
    For even $m$, pairing consecutive rounds and summing yields
    $\sum_{i=1}^{m} n_{B_i}\geq (m/2)b$.
\end{proof}

\begin{proposition}\label{prop:even-m}
\begin{equation}
    b\leq \frac{4t}{m} \quad (m \text{ even})  \label{eq:skipping3}
\end{equation}
\end{proposition}
\begin{proof}
    Denote by $n_{B_i}$ the number of active positions of $\mathbf{B}_i$.
    The skipping trail condition (Definition~\ref{def:skipping-trail}) partitions
    the active positions of each $\mathbf{B}_i$ evenly into $L_i$ and $R_i$, so
    at most $\sum_i n_{B_i}/2$ independent degrees of freedom are introduced;
    since these must fit in an $t$-dimensional state space,
    \begin{equation}\label{eq:total-active}
        \sum_{i=1}^{m} n_{B_i} \leq 2t.
    \end{equation}
    By Corollary~\ref{cor:consecutive}, $n_{B_i}+n_{B_{i+1}}\geq b$ for all $i$.
    For even $m$, pairing consecutive terms gives $\sum_{i=1}^{m} n_{B_i}\geq (m/2)b$.
    Combining with~\eqref{eq:total-active}:
    \[
        \frac{m}{2}\,b \;\leq\; \sum_{i=1}^{m} n_{B_i} \;\leq\; 2t,
    \]
    which yields $b\leq 4t/m$.
\end{proof}


\begin{proposition}
    If the linear transformation $\LLL$ is MDS, then a 4-round skipping trail cannot exist.
\end{proposition}
\begin{proof}
    Follows from the fact that for an MDS matrix, the branch number is maximal $t+1$, and thus the condition for a 4-round skipping trail would
    be $t+1\leq \frac{4t}{4}$, which is not satisfied for any $t\geq 1$.
\end{proof}
\begin{remark}
   Four rounds of an MDS-based SPN structure can potentially be skipped if the differential for the first round is relaxed.
\end{remark}
